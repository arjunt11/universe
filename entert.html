Re-releases Will Continue To Dominate Box Office; A Look At What's In Store For Bollywood

'Ranbir Kapoor's Rockstar Turned The Tide' - Joginder Tuteja

It's a completely new experience to watch your favourite stars return to the big screen, to sing along the songs which you may have heard a million times already. Even when you know the ending of Veer-Zaara, the first scene and the last will still bring tears to your eyes. Theatre chains across India are currently bringing that magic to the big screen. Re-releasing of older Bollywood releases has always been on occasion, for celebration but it has evolved into something new this season. Classics like Gangs of Wasseypur, Veer Zara, Rehna Hai Tere Dil Mein and many more are returning to the big screen this week alone. Some recent anticipated tiles have been Tumbbad, and Laila Majnu gave fans a chance to enjoy the stories they may have missed the first time. With a re-release, the latter also found its massive audience base, despite mixed reviews after the original release. To understand this trend, we got in touch with some of the industry experts and the minds behind the decisions of such re-releases.
